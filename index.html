SimLauncher: Launching Sample-Efficient Real-world Robotic Reinforcement Learning via Simulation Pre-training

Abstract

SimLauncher presents a novel approach to sample-efficient robotic reinforcement learning through simulation pre-training. Our method leverages large-scale simulation environments to pre-train robotic policies before transferring them to real-world scenarios.

By utilizing advanced domain adaptation techniques and curriculum learning strategies, SimLauncher achieves significant improvements in sample efficiency compared to traditional reinforcement learning approaches. The framework demonstrates robust performance across various manipulation tasks including pick-and-place, insertion, and dexterous grasping.

Our experimental results show that SimLauncher reduces the required real-world training samples by up to 80% while maintaining high task success rates. This breakthrough enables more practical deployment of robotic systems in real-world applications where data collection is expensive and time-consuming.
