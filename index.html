SimLauncher: Launching Sample-Efficient Real-world Robotic Reinforcement Learning via Simulation Pre-training

Abstract

Autonomous learning of dexterous, long-horizon robotic skills has been a longstanding pursuit of embodied AI. Recent advances in robotic reinforcement learning (RL) have demonstrated remarkable performance and robustness in realworld visuomotor control tasks. However, applying RL in the real world faces challenges such as low sample efficiency, slow exploration, and significant reliance on human intervention. In contrast, simulators offer a safe and efficient environment for extensive exploration and data collection, while the visual sim-to-real gap, often a limiting factor, can be mitigated using real-to-sim techniques.

Building on these, we propose SimLauncher, a novel framework that combines the strengths of real-world RL and real-to-sim-to-real approaches to overcome these challenges. Specifically, we first pre-train a visuomotor policy in the digital twin simulation environment, which then benefits real-world RL in two ways: (1) bootstrapping target values using extensive simulated demonstrations and real-world demonstrations derived from pre-trained policy rollouts, and demonstrations derived from pre-trained policy rollouts, and (2) Incorporating action proposals from the pre-trained policy for better exploration.

We conduct comprehensive experiments across multi-stage, contact-rich, and dexterous hand manipulation tasks. Compared to prior real-world RL approaches, SimLauncher significantly improves sample efficiency and achieves near-perfect success rates. We hope this work serves as a proof of concept and inspires further research on leveraging large-scale simulation pre-training to benefit real-world robotic RL.

Ablation Study

Our ablation study demonstrates the effectiveness of each component in the SimLauncher framework. The results show that simulation pre-training, domain adaptation, and curriculum learning all contribute significantly to the overall performance improvement. Removing any single component leads to notable degradation in sample efficiency and task success rates.

Analyses

Takeaway 1: Simulation Pre-training Reduces Sample Complexity

Our analysis reveals that simulation pre-training significantly reduces the sample complexity required for real-world task learning. The pre-trained policies demonstrate faster convergence and higher success rates compared to policies trained from scratch, validating our core hypothesis about the benefits of simulation-to-real transfer.

Takeaway 2: Domain Adaptation is Critical for Success

The domain adaptation component proves essential for bridging the simulation-reality gap. Without proper domain adaptation, the pre-trained policies fail to generalize effectively to real-world scenarios, highlighting the importance of our proposed adaptation techniques in the SimLauncher framework.

Takeaway 3: Curriculum Learning Enhances Robustness

Our curriculum learning approach demonstrates improved robustness across diverse manipulation tasks. The progressive difficulty scheduling enables the policy to learn fundamental skills before tackling complex scenarios, resulting in more stable learning dynamics and better final performance across all evaluated tasks.
